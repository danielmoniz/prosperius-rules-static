Prosper

Prosper Phase

Your villagers now get to eat (hopefully!) and prosper.

  1. Subtract Prosperity based on your current Prosperity score. The amounts are on the Prosperity score board.

  2. Gain one Prosperity for each needed/wanted good by your villagers. All such goods should be lined up beside your population.

    • Eg. 1 point for each:
      • quality food consumed
      • clothing worn
      • wine drunk
      • ...Etc!
    • Refer to the reference card if unsure about which goods your villagers need/want.

  3. Discard all food eaten this round.

  4. Discard any quality food in your stockpile - it has spoiled.

  5. Discard one clothing.

Food

There are two types of food in Prosperius: quality and non-quality food.

Each quality food consumed generates a point and prevents starvation.
  • They are discarded at the end of the round if stored in your stockpile (spoilage).
Each non-quality food consumed only prevents starvation.
  • Bread and Wheat are the only types of non-quality food. However, Bread is special.

Bread is Special

Bread was the base of nearly every meal for medieval Europeans. To represent this, each quality food consumed makes one consumed bread count as quality food.

Note that your villagers still prefer quality food over bread when it comes to trading. See Food Demand.

Examples

Your village has 5 population.

  1. They all consume bread
    • => counts as 0 quality food (no quality food)
  2. One villager consumes fish/fruit/etc., the other 4 consume bread
    • => counts as 2 quality food (1 quality food + 1 bread)
  3. Two villagers consume fish/fruit/etc., the other 3 consume bread
    • => counts as 4 quality food (2 quality food + 2 bread)
  4. They all consume fish/fruit/etc.
    • => counts as 5 quality food (all quality food)

Starvation

If any of your population fails to eat, they will starve next round and your village immediately loses Prosperity.

For each population that does not eat:

  • -1 Prosperity immediately

Moreover, if ANY of your population does not eat, place aside one villager next round as Starving. Starving villagers:

  • Cannot perform actions
  • Eat and demand as normal

If all of your workers eat, you will not have any Starving villagers on the following round. You should never have more than one Starving villager.

Luxury Goods

Luxury goods generally provide +1 Prosperity for consumption. Note, however, that:

  • Luxury clothing counts as clothing and a luxury good. It therefore provides +2 Prosperity.
  • Luxury food counts as food and a luxury good. It therefore provides +2 Prosperity.

Examples

  • A Tier 3 village has four population (-8). Three of them eat quality food (+3), and one eats non-quality food (+0). They are all clothed (+4) and two are educated (+2).
    • This amounts to +1 Prosperity total (9-8).

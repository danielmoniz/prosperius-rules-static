Terminology

Terminology

Population/worker/villager

One of your 5 playing pieces. The term workers is generally used as population in the context of taking actions, ie. chopping down trees.

Thematically, each villager represents a number of people.

Visitor

A visitor is a villager from a village other than your own. For example, the School gives Prosperity to its owner for each visitor use. This only provides Prosperity when used by villagers belonging to other players (not your own).

Complete (location)

A location on your player board that has been flipped face-up (after having all required resources placed on it). Your Village Centre is a completed location, but your Gather card is not (it is not on the player board).

Under Construction/Incomplete

A location that is face-down. It has been started, but has not yet been completed by flipping it over to be face-up.

Discard

Put back into the central supply of goods or cards.

Location

Any card that can be placed on your player board. Eg. a Farm and a Smithy are both Locations.

Central Supply

Refers to either the cards in the middle or the goods in the bank (belonging to no player), depending on context.

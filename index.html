Introduction

Prosperius - Rules

Introduction

In Prosperius, each player acts as a Mayor that guides their fledgling village to become a thriving town. Players build locations and take actions to create valuable trade goods. Trade is non-optional in Prosperius; your villagers will trade goods if they need them or have surplus. The more prosperous a village, the more people populate it - and the more its citizens demand. The player who develops the most prosperous village wins!

Overview

Prosperius is a game of worker placement and trade. Players take turns placing their workers, also known as their villagers or population, on actions such as Build and Gather. As a village grows, so too does its available actions. Each new location provides a new action that can be performed.

As a village consumes and trades goods, it becomes more prosperous. Increasing in prosperity causes a player to gain Prosperity (points), thereby increasing in tier. Each increase in tier raises the village’s population, unlocks new locations that can be built, and places greater demands on the growing village.

Game End

The game ends when any player has at least 100 Prosperity points at the end of a round. The player with the most Prosperity wins! If there is a tie, the tied players share in a victory.

Modes of play/number of players

Prosperius is designed for 2-5 players. The game can be played competitively against each other with all amounts of players, but a two-player game plays best in Cooperative Mode.
